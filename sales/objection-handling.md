# CSTAR Media — Objection Handling Cheat Sheet

Print this and keep it next to you during every call.

---

## OBJECTION 1: "It's too expensive"

**What they mean:** They don't see enough value yet.

**Response:**
> "I totally get that — $1,500 is real money. Can I ask — what does a new customer typically spend with you over their lifetime?"

[They answer]

> "So if I bring you 10 new customers this month, that's [$X] in revenue for a $1,500 investment. That's the math we're working with. Does that feel like a better way to look at it?"

**If still resistant:**
> "What would the investment need to look like for it to make sense?"

---

## OBJECTION 2: "I need to think about it"

**What they mean:** Something is unclear or they're not sold yet.

**Response:**
> "Totally fair. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think through? I'd rather answer any questions now than have you sitting on it."

[Listen carefully]

> "Is it more about the money, or is there something about the approach that's not sitting right?"

**If vague:**
> "Here's what I'll do — I'll follow up in [2 days]. If after that it's still not a yes, no hard feelings at all. Does that work?"

---

## OBJECTION 3: "We already have someone doing our ads"

**What they mean:** Status quo feels safer.

**Response:**
> "That's great — how's it going with them? Are you hitting the lead numbers you want?"

[If yes → congratulate and move on]

[If no or uncertain:]
> "What results are you currently getting — leads per month, cost per lead, that kind of thing?"

> "I'm not here to bash anyone else's work, but if the results aren't where you need them, it might be worth at least seeing what a different approach looks like. Would a free audit be valuable — just so you have something to compare?"

---

## OBJECTION 4: "We do our own marketing in-house"

**What they mean:** They think they can handle it themselves.

**Response:**
> "That's awesome — what platforms are you running on right now?"

> "What kind of results are you seeing — leads per month, cost per lead?"

[If struggling:]
> "Here's the thing — running ads in-house sounds good in theory, but the algorithm changes constantly and it's easy to burn budget without knowing why. I specialize in this one thing every single day. What would it mean for your team if they got that time back?"

---

## OBJECTION 5: "The timing isn't right"

**What they mean:** Something else is taking priority or they're scared.

**Response:**
> "I hear you — when do you think the timing would be better?"

[Get a specific timeframe]

> "What needs to happen between now and [timeframe] for it to make sense?"

> "The only thing I'd say is — every month you're not running ads is a month your competitors might be. But I'd rather you start when you're ready than start half-committed. Can I follow up with you on [specific date]?"

- 30–60% → Healthy range. Keep going.
